Tenor Trombone

$600

  1. Most common type the tenor trombone is the most frequently used trombone in various genres of music, including classical, jazz, and marching bands.
  2. Slide mechanism It features a slide mechanism that allows players to change the pitch by extending or retracting the slide. This provides flexibility and precise intonation control.
  3. Versatile range The tenor trombone has a wide range, allowing players to perform both low and high notes with relative ease.
  4. Compact size Compared to bass and valve trombones, the tenor trombone is relatively compact and lightweight, making it suitable for players of different ages and skill levels.

bass Trombone

$900

  1. Lower pitch range The bass trombone is larger and produces a lower sound than the tenor trombone, making it ideal for playing lower harmonies and bass lines.
  2. Dual triggers It typically features two triggers on the slide, allowing players to extend the range and access additional low notes with ease.
  3. Rich and powerful sound The bass trombone produces a rich and powerful sound that adds depth and resonance to brass ensembles and orchestras.
  4. Specialized roles Due to its lower pitch range and larger size, the bass trombone is often utilized in jazz, symphony orchestras, and other ensembles where a deep, supporting voice is needed.

Valve Trombone

$1200

  1. Valves for pitch control Unlike the slide mechanism of the tenor and bass trombones, the valve trombone features piston valves, similar to those found on a trumpet or a euphonium. This allows for faster and more precise pitch control.
  2. Compact design The valve trombone has a more compact design compared to the tenor and bass trombones, making it suitable for players who prefer a smaller instrument or need increased maneuverability.
  3. Ease of playability The valve trombone is often considered easier to play for beginners or those transitioning from valve-based instruments, as it requires less air and finger dexterity to produce notes.
  4. Unique sound The valve trombone has a distinct sound characterized by a smooth and mellow tone, often used in jazz and traditional Dixieland music.
